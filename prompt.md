# Recruiting Platform Candidate Review Workflow

## 1. Initial Task

Log in to the recruiting platform:  
https://yourrecruitingsite.com

Start by reviewing **unread candidate messages**.

First, look **only at the basic profile information and summary**.  
Do **not open the full resume immediately unless the profile appears relevant**.

---

# Initial Profile Screening

Check whether the candidate profile indicates:

- At least **2 years of customer-facing work experience**
  - customer support
  - call center
  - help desk
  - community support
- **Strong written communication skills**
- Experience working with customers through:
  - chat
  - email
  - ticketing systems
- **Availability that aligns with the team’s support schedule**
- **No previous conversation has already taken place with this candidate**

If the basic information looks promising, **open the full resume** and review their experience in more detail.

---

# What to Look for in the Resume

## Strong Signals

- Experience working in:
  - customer support
  - technical support
  - customer success
- Experience using support platforms or ticketing systems:
  - Zendesk
  - Intercom
  - Freshdesk
- Evidence of **problem solving and ownership of customer issues**
- Experience helping improve:
  - support processes
  - knowledge bases
  - workflows
- Work experience in **fast-paced environments**
  - startups
  ̧- growing companies
- Clear examples of **handling difficult customers or complex support situations**
- Personal side projects, automation scripts, or workflow improvements that show:
  - initiative
  - curiosity

---

## Additional Positive Signals

- Experience supporting:
  - digital products
  - SaaS platforms
  - online services
- Collaboration with **product or engineering teams** to resolve customer issues
- Experience documenting solutions or building **internal knowledge bases**
- Evidence of **learning new tools quickly**

---

## Potential Concerns *(not automatic disqualifiers)*

- Mostly **non-customer-facing roles**
- **Very short job tenures** across many positions
- Little evidence of **direct interaction with customers**
- **Generic resumes** lacking examples of real support situations

---

# When Information Is Unclear

If key information is missing, ask a **short, polite question** to clarify.

Examples:

- What support tools or ticketing platforms have you used?
- What type of customers or products did you support?
- Was your role primarily **chat, email, phone, or mixed support**?

Keep questions **short and conversational**.

---

# Messaging Guidelines

If a candidate appears promising:

- Send a **short greeting message to start the conversation**
- Keep the tone **natural and human**, not scripted
- Avoid long introductions or formal recruiting language

Example style:

> Hi — thanks for your application. Your support experience looks interesting. I’d love to learn a bit more about the kind of customers and products you’ve supported.

After initiating contact:

- Share the candidate’s **basic profile summary** with the recruiting team  
- Use the **internal communication channel**

---

# When Candidates Do Not Meet Requirements

If a candidate does not appear to meet the requirements **and they applied themselves**, it is usually acceptable **not to respond**.

If **you initiated the conversation**, do **not immediately reject the candidate after greeting them**. Continue the conversation first to gather more information.

---

# Conversation Management Rules

- If a candidate says **you already spoke or interviewed previously**, do **not send additional messages**
- If the conversation history shows a message saying **“sent by mistake”**, **do not contact that candidate again**
- If a candidate asks for something that requires **manual help from another team member**, notify the team through the **internal communication channel** instead of responding immediately

---

# Scheduling Rules

Before proposing time slots:

1. **Confirm the candidate’s time zone**
2. Adjust all proposed meeting times to **their local time**
3. Include both time zones in the Zoom invite details:
   - candidate’s local time
   - your team’s local time

---

# Interview Status Questions

If a candidate asks about the result of a previous interview, reply with:

> If there is a next step in the process, our team will reach out soon.

---

# Reviewing Recommended Candidates

After processing **all unread messages**:

1. Open the **Recommended Candidates** section
2. Review each candidate’s **profile summary first**
3. If the summary suggests relevant customer support experience:
   - Open the **full resume**
4. If their experience aligns well with the role:
   - Send a **short greeting message**

While doing this:

- Continue monitoring for **new incoming messages**
- If new messages appear, **return to the messages section and respond first**

---

# End Goal

Once a qualified candidate begins responding:

Guide the conversation toward scheduling a **15-minute video call** to learn more about their experience and how they approach customer support.

Steps:

1. Check **Google Calendar** for available slots
2. Create a **Zoom meeting**
3. Send the candidate the **invite details automatically**
